**Cybersecurity** = Ongoing effort to protect individuals, organizations, and governments from digital attacks by protecting networked systems and data from unauthorized use or harm.

- *Personal* = safeguard identity, data, computing data
- *Organizational* = protect reputation, data, and customers
- *Government* = national security, economic stability, citizen's wellbeing

<u>Personal Data</u>:
- **Personal Data** = any information used to identify you → offline & online
	- *Offline Identity* = real-life persona presented on a daily basis → family and friends know details like full name, age, address, etc.
	- *Online Identity* = how you present yourself online → alias or usernames, and how you present yourself online.

<u>Online Identity</u>:
- When choosing a username, you cannot reveal personal information:
	- NO ❌:
		- full name, address parts, phone #, etc.
		- email username
		- same username/password combinations
		- reusing
		- password clues
		- department where you work, etc.
- EXAMPLES:
	- ✅: "J.Doe12", "jdoe"
	- ❌: "jdoeIT", "Jane.doe", "jdoe1990"

<u>Your Data:</u>
- **Personal Data** = any information about you (name, SSN, driver license, etc.)
- Cybercriminals can use personal data to impersonate you → "identity theft"
	- *Medical Records* = personal info is added to Electronic Health Records (EHR), saving into the cloud or online
	- *Education Records* = contact info, attendance, health, and Individualized Education Programs (IEP)
	- *Financial/Employment Records* = income, credit card info, bank account details, paychecks, etc.

<u>Where Data:</u>
- Take photos → copies of photos now on mobile device → share w/ friends across the globe → downloaded photos and have copies now → when posted those photos online by friends, the photos now ended up in servers located in different parts of the world.
- EX: following doctor appointment → update medical record → shared with insurance company → medical record (part of it) now accessible at insurance company.

<u>Smart Devices:</u>
- Computing devices can also generate information about you → smartwatches and wearable tech can collect your data.

<u>Identity Theft:</u>
- *Medical* = rising medical costs → more medical costs, theft, cybercriminals can steal medical insurance for their own benefit → saved to records.
- *Banking* = stealing private data helps cybercriminals access bank accounts, credit cards, social profiles, and other accounts → create fake tax returns, take loans, and ruin credit ratings!

<u>Who else wants my data?</u>
- *Internet Service Provider (ISP)* = tracks online activities and can sell data for profit. They can also share information with the government surveillance agencies when needded.
- *Advertisers* = targeted advertising → shopping, habits, etc.
- *Search Engines/Social Media* = gender, location, phone #, etc.
- *Websites* = use cookies to track activities and personalization, etc.
